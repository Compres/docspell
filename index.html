… but find them if you need

The everyday life has to deal with quite some amount of papers that are getting to us via postal mail. Some of them are important, we should keep them. But most you’ll never need again. And once in a while we are forced to dig out this one document….

Docspell can help out here. It aids in stowing the document away: scan it and throw the paper away (most of the time anyways). And it helps getting them back out: the text is analyzed and documents are automatically tagged. This process is not 100%, but it works quite well.

Docspell consists of two parts: The REST server that provides a REST api and a web application. The job executor (short joex), processes the documents (extracts the text and analyzes it). You can run multiple rest servers and job executors to share the work across machines. It also requires a SQL database, but can run with a file-based one such as H2.

How it works

This is the basic concept:

  • You have to “maintain” a kind of address book. It should list the name of the correspondents and the concerning people/things.
  • Push your documents to docspell. There are some ways which are described in the user documentation.
  • Docspell then analyzes the contents and tries to find matches within your address book. It can detect the correspondent and often enough a concerning person or thing. It will then associate this data to your documents.
  • You can then inspect what docspell has done and correct it. If docspell has found more than one suggestions, they will be given to you. Very often the correct one is just one click away.

If your documents are tagged that way, it shouldn’t be hard to find them. Addtional to the things docspell tries to find, you can tag and “by hand”, too.
